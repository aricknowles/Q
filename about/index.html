Forward

American Typewriter was created as an ode to typewriter fonts. It makes a compromise between the rigidity of the typefaces used in typewriters and the expectations of being used in digital medium, the font retains the typical typewriter alphabet forms, lending the font to a hint of nostalgia. I choose to focus on the “Q” in the light weight of this family, being that it is whimsical, with its curvy tail that loops back inside the q, causing it to only have one end. On top of this, since it is a monotype, being in its light weight it has a feeling of weightlessness, almost feeling as though the width of the line never changes. This is what enticed me into working with it.

During the course of the project, I worked with different ways of speaking through the letter in 3D materials and Photoshop. How do you go against what the letter is inherently, while still maintaining its form? What materials directly speak to it? How far does it need to be pushed before it starts speaking as something else? Or starts speaking as anything at all? These are the questions I attempted to answer while creating the Q in its various forms. Presented here is a collection of those answers.→
